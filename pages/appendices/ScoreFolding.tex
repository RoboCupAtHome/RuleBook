\section*{Definition}

Folding a t-shirt means taking a flat, spread-out shirt and folding one part over another along an intended fold line, reducing the shirt size in at least one dimension.

A fold is considered successful if:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item the folded section lies flat without significant bunching,
    \item edges are reasonably aligned, and
    \item no major unintended creases or folds appear outside the intended fold line.
\end{itemize}

A folding task consists of a sequence of folds resulting in a neat, approximately rectangular shape.


\section*{Folding Quality Evaluation}
\textbf{Folding quality is evaluated based on neatness, flatness, and alignment}

All percentages in this appendix refer to the maximum available folding score. Assistance penalties are applied as deductions from the maximum folding score, independent of the achieved folding quality score.

Penalties for human assistance during folding are proportional to the amount of help provided:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Minimal help} (e.g., smoothing wrinkles, stabilizing cloth): small penalty ($\sim$12.5 percentage points).
    \item \textbf{Partial folding} (e.g., folding one half or aligning edges): moderate penalty ($\sim$25 percentage points).
    \item \textbf{Major help} (e.g., completing entire fold/stack): maximum penalty.
\end{itemize}

The penalties are indicative examples and referees may adjust penalties proportionally to the level of assistance provided.
